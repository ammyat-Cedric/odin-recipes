Carbonara

Description

Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish originating from Rome. It typically consists of spaghetti tossed with a rich sauce made from eggs, Parmesan cheese, and cured pork (traditionally guanciale or pancetta).

The dish is known for its creamy texture, achieved without the use of heavy cream, and its bold, savory flavors. Carbonara is often served with black pepper and additional Parmesan cheese, offering a simple yet indulgent culinary experience.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti (340g)
  • Three large eggs
  • Parmesan cheese (250g, grated)
  • Bacon (6 slices)
  • Salt and Black pepper
  • 2 cloves of Garlic (minced)
  • Fresh Parsley

Instructions

  1. Cook the spaghetti
  2. Prepare the Bacon
  3. Saute garlic
  4. Make the sauce
  5. Combine the pasta and sauce
  6. Serve and enjoy!
